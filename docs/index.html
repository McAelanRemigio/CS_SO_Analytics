Workforce & Labor Market Intelligence System

A data-driven labor market analytics framework supporting a multi-institutional California State University initiative tied to a $2M workforce development grant proposal.

Public Sector Analytics • Workforce Systems • Policy Modeling

Executive Summary

This project translated fragmented labor market and employer data into a unified decision system for workforce alignment, supporting grant justification and regional education-to-employment strategy design.
Problem

Disconnected workforce pipelines across CSU institutions and unclear employer demand signals.

Solution

Built a labor market intelligence system combining job postings, classification models, and survey analytics.

Impact

Enabled data-backed workforce alignment strategy used in a $2M grant proposal.

Key Insights

Demand Concentration

Labor demand clusters heavily in a small set of technical entry-level roles.

Pipeline Misalignment

Traditional CSU pipelines under-supply high-accessibility technical roles.

Employer Signal Strength

Internship and engagement signals outperform direct hiring signals in predictive value.

Methodology

Labor Market Modeling

Job posting aggregation, deduplication, role classification, and accessibility scoring.

Pipeline Analysis

Modeled entry pathways across internship → entry-level → experienced roles.

Survey Intelligence System

Structured employer survey analytics for engagement and hiring intent classification.

Technical Implementation

Data Processing

Python (Pandas, NumPy), regex pipelines, structured cleaning workflows.

Modeling

Rule-based NLP classification and accessibility scoring heuristics.

Outputs

CSV reporting pipelines and grant-ready analytical summaries.

Impact

This system directly supported a multi-institution workforce development grant (~$2M scope), enabling data-driven justification of employer demand gaps and intervention strategies.
Policy Contribution

Supported CSU-wide workforce alignment strategy.

Analytical Output

Decision Support

Enabled scenario-based workforce intervention modeling.
