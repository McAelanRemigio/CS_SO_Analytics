Workforce & Labor Market Intelligence Report

Portfolio Demonstration Version

Core Contribution:
Led labor market and workforce analytics supporting a multi-institutional California State University initiative contributing to a $2M workforce development grant proposal submitted under California Employment Development Department funding (U.S. Department of Labor aligned programs).

Executive Summary: Workforce Analytics Impact

This project centered on translating raw labor market data into actionable workforce insights to inform regional education-to-employment alignment strategy. The analysis directly supported grant development efforts across four California State University institutions by quantifying demand gaps, employer hiring patterns, and accessibility constraints in manufacturing and technical labor markets.

  • Modeled employer demand signals from structured job posting datasets
  • Built role classification system for workforce segmentation (engineering, operations, supply chain, etc.)
  • Developed accessibility scoring framework to estimate entry pathways across roles
  • Quantified demand–supply gaps to support funding justification narratives
  • Produced scenario-based policy simulations for workforce interventions

Labor Market Modeling & Grant Support Analysis

The following analytical framework was used to estimate workforce demand, categorize job roles, and model intervention scenarios that informed grant justification materials.

  • Job posting aggregation and deduplication across platforms
  • Standardization of salary and employer metadata
  • Role family classification using rule-based NLP heuristics
  • Pipeline-level inference (internship → entry → experienced)
  • Accessibility scoring to approximate workforce entry feasibility
